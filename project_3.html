More about me


My name is Carl Strümpfer. My surname is German, although I am not, and it translates to ‘socks’, which
I coincidentally enjoy wearing on cold days. I live on the very bottom of the African continent, in
South Africa.

I am also currently a Multimedia Design student at the Stellenbosch Academy of Design and Photography,
which to conserve oxygen, we all simply call the Academy. What you see here is some of the cool stuff
that I’ve made there.


I find myself intrigued by the concept of light, and how it dances around objects and plays with the world
around us. I make it my mission in life to capture the game between light and darkness, by any means
possible, be it photo, video or film.

I can keep myself busy with my myriad of interests, that range from photography to restoring old
technological relics, like keyboards and reel-to-reel tape decks. I also suffer from a severe case of
retro 80’s nostalgia, which influences much of what I own and listen to.

Music Peace Love

Outline

A tribute is created in order to celebrate the life of an individual of note. As is the case of Jimi Hendrix, in honour of which this tribute website was created. It follows his life and career through its high’s and low’s, in a uniquely personal manner.

Concept

An important concept from the start of this project was to humanize Hendrix, not to detract from his stardom, but to show the kind of person he truly was. The title of the website, Music, Peace and Love, echoes this very well. The last interview that Hendrix did, shortly before his death, formed the backbone for the entire layout of the website.

Execution

Every defining event in Hendrix’s life is represented along a line running through the entire website, with each stemming off to describe it in detail. These descriptions are linked to a quote from the interview, highlighted by the deep purple and bright yellow that adorned one of The Jimi Hendrix Experience’s albums.

Each event is flanked by an image that is artificially colorized from black and white using a unique algorithm.

The choice of type is such that it will not only appeal to the contemporary eye, but also tie back to a truly 1970’s aesthetic. Life events are interspersed with handwritten notes, letters and poems by Hendrix himself, that tell a deeper story and show an intimate side of the artist.
