More about me


My name is Carl Strümpfer. My surname is German, although I am not, and it translates to ‘socks’, which
I coincidentally enjoy wearing on cold days. I live on the very bottom of the African continent, in
South Africa.

I am also currently a Multimedia Design student at the Stellenbosch Academy of Design and Photography,
which to conserve oxygen, we all simply call the Academy. What you see here is some of the cool stuff
that I’ve made there.


I find myself intrigued by the concept of light, and how it dances around objects and plays with the world
around us. I make it my mission in life to capture the game between light and darkness, by any means
possible, be it photo, video or film.

I can keep myself busy with my myriad of interests, that range from photography to restoring old
technological relics, like keyboards and reel-to-reel tape decks. I also suffer from a severe case of
retro 80’s nostalgia, which influences much of what I own and listen to.

Haiku's

Outline

A haiku is an intricate form of poetry, that relies on clever use of words to tell a short story in only three lines. Similarly, motion graphics also serve to tell a short, abstract of a story.

The combination of these two arts, formed the basis of this project, where three separate motion graphics were created, based around three self-written haiku’s. Each video tells its own story, but they are visually and thematically intertwined.

Concept

Light and scale played an integral role in the production of the three videos, as can be seen in their use of tonality and contrast. Each video is presented

in three parts, that correspond to a line of their respective poems. Acts are set in an ethereal realm of mystique and uneasiness. A small cloth-like character can be seen lurking in each story, representing the viewer and how they might experience this world, constantly dwarfed by the surrounding structures.

Execution

The entirety of the series was crafted as a full three-dimensional environment, which allowed complex camera movements and a grand sense of scale. A black and white grading was chosen, to eliminate the distraction of colour, and highlight the difference between shades and not tones. The music by Mogwai is overlaid as the only source of sound in each video.
